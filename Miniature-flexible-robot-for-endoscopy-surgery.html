Miniature flexible robot for endoscopy surgery

A cutting-edge approach merging advanced imaging technologies with precise navigation systems to enhance procedural accuracy and patient outcomes. This integrated solution enables real-time visualization of internal structures via MRI, CT, or ultrasound, supporting minimally invasive interventions across cardiology, oncology, and neurosurgery.

Localization Accuracy: 31.0mm Tool: Surgical Needle/Cautery

NaviKnife

Addressing the challenge of breast tissue deformability in cancer surgery, NaviKnife is an ultrasound-guided navigation system that visualizes surgical tools relative to tumors in real time. This technology helps preserve healthy tissue while ensuring complete tumor removal.

  • Ultrasound integration for real-time tool tracking
  • Reduced positive margin rates in clinical studies
  • Decreased excised specimen weight/size
  • Enhanced tumor margin visualization

NousNav

A low-cost, open-source neuronavigation solution designed to address neurosurgeon shortages in low and middle-income countries (LMICs). By combining affordable hardware with free software, NousNav delivers essential functionalities at under $8,000 USD.

  • Open-source alternative to commercial systems
  • Collaboration between Queen’s University and Brigham and Women’s Hospital
  • Suitable for resource-constrained healthcare settings
  • Supports core neurosurgical navigation needs
Partners: Golby Lab, Kitware Initiated: 2019
3D Slicer ROS2 Real-time Visualization

Open-Source Integration

Advancing image-guided robotic systems through seamless integration of 3D Slicer (medical imaging platform) and ROS2 (Robot Operating System). The SlicerROS2 module enables direct data transfer and real-time visualization.

  • Eliminates reliance on middleware
  • Bi-directional data exchange between systems
  • Supports diverse hardware configurations
  • Part of ROSMED initiative (rosmed.github.io)
